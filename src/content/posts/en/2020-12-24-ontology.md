I bought Rain Drops' second mini-album, ["Ontology"](https://www.universal-music.co.jp/raindrops/ontology/).

The reason is a bit out of the ordinary; it's a selfish one—my name is listed in the credits because of a project I was involved with very briefly.

It’s been a little while since the release, but I suddenly realized that having something with my name on it out in the world (physically) is a rare life experience that might never happen again. I worried that my motivation was impure, but then I decided that if the alternative was regretting missing the chance a few years down the line, then being a bit impure was perfectly fine. That’s how I ended up getting it.

I liked the color scheme and layout of the cover, so I bought the First Press Limited Edition B.

As for the songs, I've been listening to them on subscription services like Spotify since they were released, so it's not like I'm hearing them for the first time. Still, I felt that the direction is different from their first mini-album, "Synesthesia," and it really shows they put a lot of thought into it.

I listen to it more during my commute than while I’m working. Perhaps it's because the chilly weather outside matches the melancholy of the songs, making it feel like I can enjoy the music even more.

That's about it.
