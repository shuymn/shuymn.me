Happy New Year. I look forward to working with you all again this year. (January 6th)

## Visiting My Parents' Home

I have been visiting my parents' house since the end of last year (because my family insisted), and I returned home on the 3rd.

My parents' house is in the countryside of Chiba. It's truly rural, with a thirty-minute walk to the nearest convenience store, and surrounded by nothing but forests and fields.

There isn't a single entertainment facility outside the house, so television and the internet are my only sources of entertainment.

It is a peaceful place, but I got bored after one day, so I spent the rest of the time working on personal development projects.

My family is currently healthy, which is the most important thing.

## Resolutions and Such

I have decided on one primary resolution (goal) for this year, which is as follows:

- Increase my output
- Lead a peaceful life

It's a fairly rough resolution, but I continue to believe that these things are important.

Regarding output, one specific vision I have is to share knowledge on one-man live streaming and recording using OBS.

Specifically, back around 2019, I focused entirely on live streaming, but I have been hoarding those experiences without sharing them at all.

Since I no longer handle those domains in my current work, my experience is becoming rusty. I think that's a waste, and given the current social climate, there might be people who need that information.

By a "peaceful life," I mean wanting to avoid letting trivial matters affect my mood and lower my performance.

Social media has been quite toxic lately, so I also want to move away from that kind of worldview.

I think Twitter may have a negative impact on both of my goals. Posting tweets makes me feel as though I’ve accomplished something, and the environment is particularly toxic.

Of course, Twitter has its merits. I just felt that, after weighing the pros and cons, it would be better for me to take a more passive, “read-only” approach.
