The 24-hour ventilation intake vent in my home (a rental) was a type that couldn't be closed, so outside noise was always slightly audible. I decided to replace it myself.

I chose a model that allows the opening to be adjusted in four stages (fully closed / slightly open / partially open / fully open).

![Temporary installation](https://i.gyazo.com/cb97fcd462f18192aa6ff1b001cb6fcb.jpg)

Since I switched from the original round type to a square one, I realized after it arrived that using caulk or similar materials would likely leave marks on the wallpaper.

For that reason, I screwed it into place by making new screw holes in a spot that (I believe) will be hidden if I switch back to the round type when I move out.

![Forced screwing (the mark at the bottom right is just a weird light reflection)](https://i.gyazo.com/22dd93e040e23e7b97f5f5e7744bacca.jpg)

Also, though I doubt it does much, I stuck some [soundproofing adhesive tape](https://www.amazon.co.jp/dp/B0091FUPUA) on the back of the panel.

![Attaching sound-absorbing material for peace of mind](https://i.gyazo.com/50f44040855a0e81d08b58fe1c41f378.jpg)

As a result, even when set to the smallest opening, more outside noise enters than before, but more air flows in as well.

I don't know how many years the previous one had been in use, but it contained a filter about 5mm thick that was quite dirty, so I assume that was restricting airflow.

When closed, very little outside noise comes in, but of course, no air comes in either, so I think it's best to only use this setting for short periods, like when I'm streaming and want to minimize outside noise.

However, since more outside noise was coming in than I expected, I bought and inserted an additional [soundproofing pipe](https://www.amazon.co.jp/dp/B00Y9RZKZ0), which is meant to be placed inside the intake vent for a bit of extra sound absorption. The ventilation volume and the amount of outside noise are now at just the right level, so I'm satisfied.

Especially this time of year, it's tough when cold air comes in during ventilation, but since ventilation isn't just for preventing sick house syndrome—it also helps lower indoor CO<sub>2</sub> levels and prevents condensation—I think I will keep it open.
