I'm writing this feeling a bit anxious about how much time has passed since my last update.

I purchased an office chair from Okamura called the [Sylphy](https://www.okamura.co.jp/product/seating/sylphy/). To be precise, I started renting it through [Kagg Rental](https://www.kagg.jp/rental/).

To give you the conclusion upfront, I am extremely satisfied with it, and as of now, I plan to continue renting it for two years and then keep it.

Originally, I was using a gaming chair from Bauhutte called the [RS-950RR](https://www.amazon.co.jp/dp/B06Y6883LY).

It balanced quality and price well, and I didn't have any major complaints. However, it didn't really feel like it fit my body well, and since I genuinely spend all day in my chair on my days off, I started thinking it wouldn't be a bad idea to buy something a little better, which is how I ended up renting this one.

When people think of office chairs, they often think of the Aeron Chair (which costs nearly 200,000 yen), but I felt the Sylphy was very well-made despite being in a price range under 100,000 yen. I think it's at a level where someone like me, stepping up from a 30,000 yen gaming chair, can really feel the improvement.

I'll list some pros and cons that come to mind.

My physical stats over the last few years are as follows:

- Height: 177cm
- Weight: 50kg

## Bauhutte RS-950RR

By the way, I bought this around this time last year, so I've been using it for about a year.

### Pros

#### 1. My back didn't hurt

I removed the lumbar and head cushions because they felt uncomfortable.

Since it was doing no active harm to my body, it had value in the sense that at least it wasn't hurting me.

#### 2. It has a headrest, so it was possible to lean back and rest my head

That said, it wasn't so important that it was a requirement.

### Cons

It feels like I have a lot of complaints when I list them out, but some points aren't really an issue if you consider it a chair for relaxing rather than a chair for working.

#### 1. I accidentally hit the caster locking function with my feet

It was slowly infuriating. It seems it was removed in the successor models, suggesting I wasn't the only one who complained.

#### 2. It barely rocks

There is a function to manually adjust the angle in stages, like a car seat, and there is a feature that technically tilts when you apply body weight, but it hardly tilted at all and I didn't feel it was practical. It might be because I'm light.

#### 3. Poor lumbar support

There was a cushion, but the incline was quite steep, and for a thin person like me, it was just a hard cushion.

I ended up using it without the cushion, which made the backrest flat and left me feeling like my lower back wasn't supported at all.

The head support couldn't be adjusted in height either, so it felt slightly misaligned, and eventually, I stopped using it altogether.

#### 4. Only felt just right at the highest seat setting

I'm 177cm tall, which was at the upper limit of the recommended height, so I suppose that's to be expected.

It seems many other people had this complaint, as the seat can be raised a bit higher in the successor models.

#### 5. The adjustable armrests are too far from the body

Because of this, it was hard to get close to the desk with the armrests raised. I thought they could have been positioned closer to the body.

I suppose I can't blame them if the reason is just that I'm too thin.

## Okamura Sylphy

Note that this is a short-term review, as I've only been sitting in it for about a week.

### Pros

#### High quality

This might be influenced by the fact that the chair I used before was a budget gaming chair, and I'm sure it depends on the color and materials, but being an office chair, I felt the design was composed and sophisticated.

The quality of the parts is also high, and I noticed the smoothness of things like the casters right away.

Some might say they don't care about these things and wish it were cheaper, and I don't want to just say "Japanese products are just different," but I really felt that a high-quality product has value in how it satisfies the desire for ownership.

#### Capable of forward-leaning posture

I don't think standard chairs follow your back when you lean forward, but this chair follows you reasonably well, providing lumbar support even when you're facing down toward the desk surface.

I have no back pain, so I'm not sure if there's a physical difference between having this and not, but even if it's not a must-have, I think it's a great feature (vague, I know).

I often face my screen, so I frequently use the back-tilt locking function.

#### Smooth reclining

I'll say it again: I'm thin, so on features that require some weight like reclining, I have to set them to the loosest possible setting to get them to move. The Sylphy feels like it is barely usable at the loosest setting, but it works. I've sat in affordable 10,000 yen chairs that rock, but although it might be obvious, this one feels smoother and tilts more naturally (Hint: Placebo effect).

#### Seat depth adjustment

It's said that it's better for the seat to have some contact with your thighs, so this feature makes it easy to adjust. I have mine set to the maximum extension.

### Cons

#### Only available with fixed headrests

I haven't bought one, so this is just speculation from someone who hasn't used one, but based on my experience with gaming chairs, I feel that a fixed headrest is a big gamble.

I think the position of the neck support is surprisingly important, and if it's in a weird spot, I feel it could actually be worse for your body.

That's about it.

I'll continue using it, and if anything else comes to mind, I intend to write about it in another post.
