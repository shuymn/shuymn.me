- DeepL
- Grammarly
- Ludwig

I signed up for annual plans for each of these.

## DeepL

I had been using this for a while. Generally, there isn't much merit in a paid plan since I translate web documents, but I thought it might be good to be able to copy and paste chunks at once and translate them loosely when I no longer feel like copying and pasting section by section. It's mostly a donation.

## Grammarly

It corrects your grammar to make it sound more natural. They officially distribute a Chrome extension, and I thought it would be useful when writing on platforms like GitHub for Issues or Pull Requests. It seems better suited for longer pieces of writing rather than short snippets like commit messages.

I just signed up and it's currently a trial period, so I can still back out, but it seems useful enough that I plan to keep paying for it.

## Ludwig

I'm not quite sure how to explain this one. It's a service where you enter an English sentence, and it finds similar sentences from sources with a high degree of credibility, such as mass media or academic papers, allowing you to compare them with your own writing (or text obtained through translation). You can also do this with Grammarly, and in reality, that might be sufficient. I also felt that some people might find the behavior of the macOS app to be a bit quirky and not to their liking.

I suppose this is useful if you’ve reached the point where you can create English text but want to improve its quality a little more.

I am also currently in the trial period for this one. It seems like less of an issue with the quality of the service and more that it doesn't really fit my use case, so unless something changes, I don't think I will continue the subscription.
