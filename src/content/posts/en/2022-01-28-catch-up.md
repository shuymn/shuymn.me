## I have a month of free time

I'll write about this in more detail on the blog when the time is right, so I'll skip it here. Please check my Twitter for the circumstances.

## I'm living a regulated life

I've been going to bed early and waking up early. Specifically, I go to sleep around 10:00 PM and wake up around 6:00 AM. I've always been a night owl, and while I never really felt that lifestyle suited me, I just ended up lazing about, which led to a lack of satisfaction with my day, staying up late for no reason, and affecting the next day—it wasn't great. Thanks to various circumstances, I've been blessed with the opportunity to maintain a regular sleep schedule for about a week now.

My impression is that my satisfaction with each day has increased significantly because I'm now able to concentrate on tasks in the morning. As a result of having more satisfying days, I've also stopped staying up late for no reason at night.

## Cleaning and ventilation

Because I'm waking up early, I've started cleaning while ventilating the room after waking up and eating breakfast. I don't like having a dirty room, so I used to clean regularly, but not every day. I feel that doing a little bit every day is better than trying to clean thoroughly once a week, as it lowers the psychological hurdle. I thought this for a while, but I've finally been able to put it into practice thanks to my regular lifestyle.

Also, I bought a CO2 sensor called CO2-mini, so I can visualize the carbon dioxide levels in my room. Now I can isolate the causes of why I might not be productive, which has resolved one of my worries. If I feel like it, I'd like to send the data to CloudWatch and set up notifications to my smartphone via Slack once it exceeds a certain level.

## Eating three meals a day

This is also a side effect of living a regulated life. Previously, I would wake up around noon and have something like a breakfast-lunch combo, so I was getting by on two meals a day plus drinks. When you wake up at 6:00 AM, it's impossible *not* to eat until 12:00 PM, so I've started eating three meals a day.

For meals, I generally stick to Nosh, but I have a 10-meal-a-week plan, and it's not enough if I eat three meals a day. So, I'm considering doing some of my own cooking, like having white rice, natto, and miso soup, or toast for breakfast. I also have some leftover mochi and pasta at home that I'd like to use up.

## Started preparing for side work

I've decided to get everything ready to start a side business, so I've been working on a few things:

- Opened a business bank account
- Applied for a business credit card (I don't know if I'll pass the screening)

Now that the groundwork is laid, I plan to file a formal business opening notification next.

By the way, since the bank required a trade name (yago) to open an account, I chose "Yamani Kenkyujo" (Yamani Laboratory). Here is why:

- English sounds stylish and smart, but since it's not my native language and I'm not proficient in it, I might fail to capture the precise nuance of the words, so I wanted to use Japanese.
- "Yamani" comes from my real name. While "Yamani" is rare as a person's name, it's relatively common as a trade name and sounds Japanese, so I wanted to use it as is if possible.
  - I've heard it's considered lucky, but there's no real information when I search for it, so the reliability is questionable.
- By basing it on my name, I wanted to avoid the feeling of "this isn't it" after some time has passed—based on my experience using various handles on the internet over the years.
- "Yamani [Something]" is common as a trade name, but I wanted a name that wouldn't overlap when searched, if possible.
- Since I intend to do work related to software engineering, I wanted to avoid names that clearly have nothing to do with it, like "Trading Co." or "Industry."

I thought I didn't need to write this down since a trade name is rarely seen, but I'll probably forget the reasons, so I decided to leave this note for my future self.

## Changed my speakers

I switched from Yamaha HS5s to Pioneer RM-05 5-inch coaxial speakers. Furthermore, on a whim, I used the EQ function of the ADI-2 DAC FS to cut the frequency bands that are amplified by the room acoustics. I feel that setting this up had a bigger impact on the sound than simply changing the speakers. As for how I did it, I used a decibel measurement app on my smartphone set to Z-weighting and a web-based tone generator app to level it out roughly. I know I should use proper measuring equipment like SoundID Reference, but I don't want to spend money that quickly, so I've settled on this for now.
