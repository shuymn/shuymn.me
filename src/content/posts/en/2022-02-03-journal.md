I received my third dose of the COVID-19 vaccine. I had received my vaccination ticket from the municipal government late last month. I had read some vague information suggesting that mixing and matching vaccines was better, so I looked for a Pfizer appointment, but couldn't find one. In the meantime, the number of COVID infections in Tokyo hit 20,000 cases per day, so I booked a Moderna appointment since they were easier to get. Incidentally, as I booked this only yesterday, it seems that depending on your municipality and the timing, you can get it done quite promptly.

Since I had strong side effects from both the first and second doses—running a fever of nearly 40°C (104°F) and being bedridden for two or three days—I was feeling a bit down thinking that this time would likely be the same.

I went to a hospital in Kudanshita, and I feel every time that it's a hassle to get from Akihabara to Kudanshita (if you're taking the subway). The Chiyoda City Office main building is also in Kudanshita, so I end up pondering this problem about once a year.
